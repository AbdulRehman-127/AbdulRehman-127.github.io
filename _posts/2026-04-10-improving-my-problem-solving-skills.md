# Improving My Problem-Solving Skills Through Programming Fundamentals

## Understanding the Importance of Problem Solving

After learning the basic concepts of Programming Fundamentals, I started focusing on something even more important: problem solving. In the beginning, I believed programming was mainly about writing code and remembering syntax. However, as the semester progressed, I realized that programming is actually a way of thinking. Every program begins with a problem, and the real challenge is finding a logical solution before writing any code.

During our lectures, Dr. Bilal Ahmad frequently emphasized that a programmer should first understand the problem and then think about the solution. He often explained that coding is only the final step of a much larger process. This advice helped me change my approach toward assignments and lab tasks. Instead of immediately opening the compiler and writing code, I started spending time understanding the requirements and planning my solution.

At first, this approach felt slow, but over time I noticed that my programs became more organized and contained fewer mistakes. I learned that when a problem is divided into smaller parts, it becomes much easier to solve. This method improved my confidence and reduced the fear I previously had when facing difficult programming questions.

Programming also helped me develop logical thinking skills that extended beyond academics. I started analyzing everyday situations in a more structured way. Whether solving a technical problem or planning a task, I found myself applying the same problem-solving techniques that I learned through programming exercises. This showed me that Programming Fundamentals was not only teaching coding but also teaching a valuable way of thinking.

---

## Learning Through Practice and Debugging

One of the biggest lessons I learned during the semester was the importance of practice. Programming is not a subject that can be mastered by reading notes alone. Every concept requires implementation and experimentation. I spent many hours solving small programming exercises and reviewing examples discussed in class.

Debugging was another major part of my learning experience. In the beginning, I became frustrated whenever my code produced errors. Sometimes a single mistake would prevent the entire program from working correctly. However, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to see debugging as an opportunity to learn rather than a sign of failure.

Following this advice, I began carefully analyzing error messages and checking my code step by step. This habit improved my patience and attention to detail. Every mistake taught me something new, and every corrected error increased my understanding of programming concepts.

Through continuous practice, I also developed better logical thinking skills. I became more comfortable with loops, conditions, functions, and problem analysis. Gradually, I noticed that I could solve programming questions much faster than before. This improvement was not the result of talent alone but the outcome of regular effort and consistent practice.

Another important realization was that every programmer learns at a different pace. There were times when I compared myself with classmates who appeared to understand concepts more quickly. However, I eventually learned that improvement comes from persistence rather than comparison. By focusing on my own progress and continuously practicing, I was able to overcome many challenges that initially seemed impossible.

---

## The Influence of Dr. Bilal Ahmad on My Learning Journey

Throughout the semester, Dr. Bilal Ahmad played an important role in shaping my understanding of programming. His teaching style focused on concept building rather than memorization. Instead of asking students to remember solutions, he encouraged us to understand the reasoning behind them.

One thing that inspired me was how he connected Programming Fundamentals with advanced technologies. As an expert in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning, Dr. Bilal Ahmad often discussed how strong programming skills are essential for developing intelligent systems. He explained that modern AI applications depend on logical thinking, data processing, and problem-solving abilities.

He also highlighted the importance of working with meaningful datasets, especially medical datasets, because they are collected with high precision and accuracy. These discussions helped me understand that the concepts we were learning in Programming Fundamentals could eventually be applied to solving real-world problems. Hearing such examples motivated me to take my studies more seriously and think about future opportunities in technology.

Looking back, this phase of my academic journey was extremely valuable. I not only improved my programming skills but also developed a stronger problem-solving mindset. The lessons I learned during this period will continue to help me in future courses and technical projects. More importantly, I now understand that success in programming comes through patience, practice, and continuous learning.

This experience strengthened my confidence and prepared me for more advanced topics that I would encounter later in my Computer Engineering journey. It also reinforced the advice repeatedly given by Dr. Bilal Ahmad: understanding the problem is always more important than rushing toward the solution. That lesson remains one of the most valuable things I learned during my first semesters.
