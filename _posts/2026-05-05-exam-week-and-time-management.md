# My Experience During Exam Week and Time Management Struggles

## The Pressure of Exam Week and Academic Load

Exam week was one of the most stressful and challenging periods of my first two semesters in Computer Engineering. During this time, everything felt overwhelming because multiple subjects required attention at the same time. Programming Fundamentals, Database Systems, and other courses like DLD and OOP all had assignments, quizzes, and preparation tasks that needed to be completed in a very short time.

I remember that during this period, my routine became extremely busy. We used to go early to classes and sometimes stayed late to complete pending work. Even basic activities like eating and resting properly became difficult because of continuous academic pressure. The workload felt heavy mainly because I had not managed my time properly earlier in the semester.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad often reminded us throughout the semester that consistency is the key to handling academic pressure. However, I realized this lesson fully during exam week when everything was coming together at once. At that time, I understood that poor time management leads to unnecessary stress and pressure.

The experience was challenging, but it also gave me a clear understanding of how important planning and discipline are in academic life.

---

## Struggles with Preparation and Last-Minute Stress

One of the biggest problems I faced during exam week was last-minute preparation. I realized that I had delayed several tasks and did not revise topics regularly. As a result, I had to cover a large amount of syllabus in a very short time, which created unnecessary stress.

Programming Fundamentals required practice, while Database Systems required conceptual understanding. Balancing both subjects at the same time was difficult. I often found myself switching between coding practice and theory revision, which made the process even more tiring.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad had already advised us to revise regularly instead of relying on last-minute preparation. He emphasized that subjects like programming and databases cannot be mastered overnight and require continuous effort. Unfortunately, I fully understood the importance of this advice only during exam week.

At times, I felt pressure and anxiety because of the workload. However, I continued revising and solving problems step by step. Slowly, I started covering important topics and became slightly more confident. This experience taught me that planning ahead is much more effective than rushing at the end.

---

## Lessons Learned in Time Management and Academic Growth

Despite the stress and difficulty, exam week became one of the most valuable learning experiences of my academic journey. It taught me the importance of time management and consistency in studies. I realized that small daily efforts are far more effective than last-minute preparation.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad’s teaching and guidance played an important role in this realization. Throughout the semester, he encouraged us to stay consistent and practice regularly in both Programming Fundamentals and Database Systems. His advice now made complete sense to me because I experienced the consequences of not following it properly.

I also learned that academic pressure is manageable if work is distributed evenly throughout the semester. Instead of leaving everything for the end, regular practice and revision can significantly reduce stress and improve performance.

This experience also helped me develop better discipline. I started understanding how to prioritize tasks and manage limited time more effectively. Even though the week was stressful, it helped me grow mentally and academically.

Looking back, exam week was not just a period of pressure but also a turning point in my learning journey. It taught me valuable life lessons about discipline, responsibility, and time management that I will carry forward in future semesters.

With continuous guidance from Dr. Bilal Ahmad and improved study habits, I now feel more prepared to handle future academic challenges in a better and more organized way.
