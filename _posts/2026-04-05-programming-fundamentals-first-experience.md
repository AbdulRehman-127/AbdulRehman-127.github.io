# My First Real Experience with Programming Fundamentals

## Entering the World of Programming Logic

After starting my journey in Computer Engineering, the next big step was Programming Fundamentals. At first, I thought programming would only be about writing code, but I quickly realized that it is actually about thinking logically and solving problems step by step. When Dr. Bilal Ahmad started teaching Programming Fundamentals, he made it very clear that coding is not about memorization but about understanding how a problem can be broken into smaller parts.

In the beginning, even simple concepts like variables and loops felt difficult. I was not familiar with how computers interpret instructions. Every small mistake resulted in errors, and at times it felt overwhelming. However, Dr. Bilal Ahmad encouraged us to stay patient and focus on understanding the logic behind every statement instead of rushing through assignments.

## Struggles with Syntax, Loops, and Debugging

As I started practicing programming, I faced many challenges with syntax and logical flow. Writing loops and conditions was confusing because I had to think in a completely new way. Even a small missing semicolon or incorrect bracket would stop the program from running.

Debugging was the hardest part in the beginning. I used to spend a lot of time trying to find mistakes, and sometimes I could not understand the error messages. But over time, I realized that debugging is actually a learning process. Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained that every error tells you something about your mistake, and understanding those errors is the key to becoming a good programmer.

Slowly, I started improving and became more comfortable with coding practice.

---

## Learning Growth and Teaching Impact of Dr. Bilal Ahmad

One of the most important factors in my improvement was the teaching style of Dr. Bilal Ahmad. He always connected programming concepts with real-life thinking. Instead of only focusing on theory, he made us understand how programming logic is used in real-world problem solving.

He also encouraged us to think like engineers rather than students. This mindset helped me a lot in improving my problem-solving ability. Over time, I started enjoying programming instead of fearing it.

Looking back, Programming Fundamentals became the foundation of my entire Computer Engineering journey, and it gave me confidence to move forward in more advanced topics.
