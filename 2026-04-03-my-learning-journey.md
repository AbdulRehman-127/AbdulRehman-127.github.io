# My Learning Journey in the First Two Semesters  
### *By Abdul Rehman*

## 1. Programming Fundamentals (PF)

### 1.1 Building My Foundation as a Programmer
Programming Fundamentals played a critical role in shaping my journey as a beginner in computer science. It introduced me to coding and logical thinking. I learned basic concepts such as variables, data types, input/output, and control structures like loops and conditionals.

At first, programming felt difficult, especially when dealing with errors. However, with practice, I started understanding how programs work. Writing small programs improved my confidence and logical thinking.

One of the most important skills I developed was breaking complex problems into smaller steps. This made problem-solving easier and more structured. I also learned the importance of writing clean and readable code.

---

### 1.2 Introduction to Machine Learning Concepts and Projects
Although Programming Fundamentals focused on basic coding, it also sparked my interest in Machine Learning. I explored simple concepts about how machines learn from data.

I realized that Machine Learning is based on logic, algorithms, and data — all connected to programming fundamentals. Even simple programs helped me understand how systems make decisions.

This motivated me to explore more advanced topics. In the future, I aim to work on real-world machine learning projects and improve my skills in this field.

---

### 1.3 Enhancing Programming Skills and Problem-Solving
Throughout the semester, I worked consistently to improve my coding skills. Practice played a major role in my progress. Solving different problems helped me develop logical and analytical thinking.

Debugging became an important skill. At first, errors were frustrating, but later I learned how to fix them effectively. This improved my patience and attention to detail.

These skills will help me in future courses and real-world development.

---

## 2. Database Management Systems (DBMS)

### 2.1 Understanding Entity-Relationship (ER) Modeling
DBMS introduced me to structured data management. One of the key concepts I learned was Entity-Relationship (ER) modeling.

Initially, it was difficult to identify entities, attributes, and relationships. However, with practice, I became confident in designing ER diagrams for systems like student records and libraries.

I also learned normalization, which reduces data redundancy and improves efficiency. This concept is essential for building strong databases.

---

### 2.2 Writing and Optimizing MySQL Queries
I learned how to write MySQL queries, starting from basic commands like SELECT, INSERT, UPDATE, and DELETE.

Later, I explored advanced queries using JOIN, GROUP BY, and ORDER BY. Combining tables helped me understand how real databases work.

This improved my problem-solving skills and gave me confidence in handling data.

---

### 2.3 Developing Practical Database Skills
I gained practical experience in creating tables, defining keys, and maintaining data integrity.

I also worked on small projects where I applied these concepts. This helped me understand how databases are used in real-world applications.

DBMS has provided me with a strong foundation in data management.

---

## 3. My Personal View and Experience

### 3.1 Importance of Libraries and Classes in Learning
I realized the importance of libraries and classes in programming. Libraries save time by providing pre-written code, while classes help organize programs.

These concepts made my coding more structured and professional. They also prepared me for advanced topics like object-oriented programming.

---

### 3.2 Challenges and Negative Experiences
My journey was not easy. I faced difficulties in understanding some concepts and managing time effectively.

The hostel environment was sometimes not suitable for studying, which affected my focus. However, these challenges taught me patience and resilience.

Mistakes helped me learn and improve.

---

### 3.3 Personal Growth and Future Development
Overall, my first two semesters were full of learning and growth. I developed discipline, time management, and problem-solving skills.

I am now more confident and focused on my goals. In the future, I plan to work on projects and improve my technical skills.

This journey is just the beginning, and I am excited for what lies ahead.
